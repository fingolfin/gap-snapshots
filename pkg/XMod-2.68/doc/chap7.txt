  [33X[0;0YThis  function  calculates  the  coproduct crossed module of crossed modules
  [22Xmathcal X_1 = (âˆ‚_1 : S_1 -> R)[122X and [22Xmathcal X_2 = (âˆ‚_2 : S_2 -> R)[122X which have

  [33X[1;0Y[29X[2XMorphismOfInducedXMod[102X( [3Xxmod[103X ) [32X attribute[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YA  morphism  of  crossed modules [22X(Ïƒ, Ï) : mathcal X_1 -> mathcal X_2[122X factors
  uniquely through an induced crossed module [22XÏ_âˆ— mathcal X_1 = (Î´ : Ï_âˆ— S_1 ->
  R_2)[122X.  Similarly,  a  morphism  of  cat1-groups  factors  through an induced
  cat1-group.  Calculation  of  induced  crossed  modules  of  [22Xmathcal  X[122X also
  provides  an  algebraic means of determining the homotopy [22X2[122X-type of homotopy
  pushouts  of  the  classifying  space of [22Xmathcal X[122X. For more background from
  algebraic topology see references in [BH78], [BW95], [BW96]. Induced crossed
  modules  and  induced  cat1-groups  also  provide  the  building  blocks for
  constructing pushouts in the categories [13XXMod[113X and [13XCat1[113X.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YData  for  the  cases  of  algebraic  interest  is provided by a conjugation
  crossed  module  [22Xmathcal  X  = (âˆ‚ : S -> R)[122X and a homomorphism [22XÎ¹[122X from [22XR[122X to a
  third  group [22XQ[122X. (It is hoped to implement more general cases in due course.)
  The output from the calculation is a crossed module [22XÎ¹_âˆ—mathcal X = (Î´ : Î¹_âˆ—S
  -> Q)[122X together with a morphism of crossed modules [22Xmathcal X -> Î¹_âˆ—mathcal X[122X.
  When  [22XÎ¹[122X is a surjection with kernel [22XK[122X then [22XÎ¹_âˆ—S = [S,K][122X (see [BH78]). When [22XÎ¹[122X
  is an inclusion the induced crossed module may be calculated using a copower
  construction [BW95] or, in the case when [22XR[122X is normal in [22XQ[122X, as a coproduct of
  crossed  modules  ([BW96],  but  not  yet  implemented). When [22XÎ¹[122X is neither a
  surjection  nor  an  inclusion,  [22XÎ¹[122X  is  factored  as  the  composite  of the
  surjection  onto the image and the inclusion of the image in [22XQ[122X, and then the
  composite  induced  crossed  module  is constructed. These constructions use
  Tietze transformation routines in the library file [10Xtietze.gi[110X.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YAs  a  first, surjective example, we take for [22Xmathcal X[122X the normal inclusion
  crossed  module  of  [10Xa4[110X  in  [10Xs4[110X, and for [22XÎ¹[122X the surjection from [10Xs4[110X to [10Xs3[110X with
  kernel [10Xk4[110X. The induced crossed module is isomorphic to [10XX3[110X.[133X
