  [1X3.1 Why are all examples in this manual over â„¤ or â„¤/mâ„¤?[0X
  
  As  the reader might notice, all examples in this manual will be either over
  â„¤  or  over  one  of its residue class rings â„¤/mâ„¤. There are two reasons for
  this.  The  first  reason  is that [5XGAP[0m does not natively support rings other
  than â„¤ in a [13Xsufficient[0m way (--> [14X1.1-2[0m).
  
  The  second  and  more  important  reason  is  to underline the fact the all
  effective  homological constructions that are relevant for [5XModules[0m have only
  as  much  to  do with the GrÃ¶bnerbasis algorithm as they do with the Hermite
  algorithm  for  the  ring  â„¤;  both algorithms are used to effectively solve
  inhomogeneous  linear  systems  over  the  respective  ring.  And [5XModules[0m is
  designed  to use rings and matrices over these rings together with all their
  operations  as  a black box. In other words: Because [5XModules[0m works for â„¤, it
  works by its design for all other computable rings.
